Technical FAQ

How Laser Kart Works

1) How does the kart know when you drive over a Powerup?

A small module mounted to the bottom of the kart detects the bright color of the Powerup icon. The icon itself does not have any electronics, RFID, or batteries. It’s totally passive. It can be as simple as a painted block on the track or a small rubber mat that can be moved around to change the track layout. The only requirement is bright color. It can be any shape, size, or image.

2) Are the lasers safe?

Yes, 100%. They aren’t technically ‘lasers’. The electronics and emitting diodes are very similar to what you would find in commercial ‘laser tag’ guns.

3) How does this system control the engine?

Small, computer controlled electric motors manipulate the throttle linkage in parallel with the gas pedal. From the engine’s perspective, it is the same as the driver taking his foot off the gas pedal during an idle or pushing the pedal down a little more for a boost. This system is totally bolt on and does not interfere with spark timing or fuel supply.

4) Are the Speed Boosts controllable once activated?

Yes, absolutely. When a ‘Speed Boost’ is activated, the engine control motors apply a little extra pressure to the throttle linkage, but this only makes a difference if the driver is pressing the gas pedal down. If the driver takes his foot off the gas, the engine will idle only slightly higher than normal. Pressing the brake pedal at any time causes the kart to stop as normal, even during a ‘Speed Boost’.

5) Won't this encourage reckless driving?

We’ve found that most aggressive driving on Go Kart tracks happens when one driver is trying to pass another driver. Because all normal go karts on a given track operate at the same speed, the only option an aggressive driver has for passing the person in front of him is bumping, cutting them off, and jamming between them and the track rail, usually while going around a corner. With Laser Kart, there are now two fun, safe, non-contact ways for drivers to pass each other. Because they can idle the other driver’s engine or speed boost around them, there is no need for bumping or ramming while going around corners! Contact like that would just slow the aggressive driver down.

6) How does the track operator keep control?

The Laser Kart system comes with an Operator Control Box that can wirelessly stop all karts on the track. When the operator activates the Stop command, all karts on the track will have their engines idled by the Laser Kart system.

7) Do you need special go karts for Laser Kart?

Nope. The Laser Kart system is totally bolt on. It can be installed on just about any type of commercial go kart with minimal modification.
